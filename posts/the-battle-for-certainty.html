The Battle for Certainty

17 May 2026

"What matters is the concept of a probability verging on certainty."

The essay sketches an imaginary cathedral. Its pillars are of causal logic, its vaults of integrated information. It is a coherent and hopeful construction. But every cathedral is built on a foundation of assumptions, and where do those come from? Given the model, something can perhaps be proven with certainty, but the model itself is at most highly probable.

The Temptation

The model I have in mind is almost identical to the Wolfram model. The only difference is the basic assumption that there are no completed infinities. In the case of the full Ruliad, the observer slices the infinite multiverse in a complex way, so the complexity is due to the observer. According to Gorard, it doesn't even matter whether all the complexity is in the universe or in the observer. According to my view, the observer and the universe are exactly the same thing on a fundamental level, but because a large experience follows the rules of IIT, many issues are observer-dependent. For example, while we sleep, reality just continues without us. In this case the difference between what we percieve and what is real is almost absolute.

The temptation is then strong to take the next step: from 'this model works as an explanation' to 'this is reality.' Herein lies the catch. Our rejection of a completed infinity is a philosophical choice, driven by taking the universe at face value. But the universe may be infinite in a way we cannot imagine. Perhaps our consciousness has structural properties that conceal the truth. The existence of the full Ruliad is not impossible.

The Limit

What matters is the concept of a probability that verges on certainty. In science and law, this is the highest attainable. DNA evidence uniquely matching a suspect, the predicted trajectory of an asteroid based on general relativity—these are not absolute certainties, but converging lines of evidence that make doubt unreasonable.

My construction is first and foremost a research project defined by internal consistency. It is this rule acting on this substrate. When analyzed correctly, it offers a hopeful, ethical framework. Today, the search space of hypergraph rules is too vast to determine our rule. We cannot calculate whether the causal structure it generates actually corresponds with observations.

Therefore I reformulate the firm conclusions of this essay here with a necessary margin of uncertainty. It is not: 'It is impossible for the Ruliad to be the foundation of reality.' It is: 'An active, causal rule is a fundamental and fruitful model for the process we experience.'

The Position

The responsible position is a fundamentally open attitude, based on the unknown. For example: nothing can ever come from nothing. Very well, let us then assume that normally nothing can appear without a cause. But what was there before the Big Bang? We don't know, but if we truly don't know, it may just as well have been nothing.

One well-documented, inexplicable phenomenon—a causal event that cannot in any way be traced back to the rule—would undermine the whole construction. To date, no such phenomenon has been found, and if that remains so, it is the true basis of the model. It is not that the universe cannot possibly be otherwise. It is that the universe, to the limits of our measurement accuracy, consistently behaves that way.
