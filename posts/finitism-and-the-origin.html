Finitism and the Origin

17 May 2026

In my essay The Cathedral of Cause and Experience, the Ruliad was dismissed on an intuitive basis. I want to elaborate on that a little by assuming moments in the past (like yesterday) did really exist. The reasoning is simple, but the conclusion is inescapable:

If the history of reality is finite, then there was nothing that preceded the first moment. The first moment must have emerged from the void, but that is illogical. The void does not exist and therefore cannot cause anything.

If the history of reality is infinite, then the series of moments that have led to the present is infinite. The series is completed in the present, but that is illogical because an infinite series cannot be completed.

If the history of reality is not finite, then it is infinite, and vice versa. It is therefore illogical that the history is both not finite and not infinite. All possibilities are thereby exhausted.

Conclusion: The origin of reality is illogical. Logic alone cannot resolve the dilemma—yet reality undeniably exists. The origin is therefore both logical and illogical. That is the "magic of the origin".

Although the origin of something usually determines its properties, the magic of the origin does not simply radiate through the whole of reality. In the infinite case, the magic is the existence of infinity itself. This leaves the laws of physics forever ungrounded, because there is always something below the smallest scale or beyond the observable universe. In the pursuit of science, we would end up as "reductionists". In the finite case, by contrast, the magic is entirely concentrated at the origin. What emerges from the void is not some arbitrary complex entity, but something minimal: a single point. At the smallest scale, there is nothing beneath, behind, or between the fundamental grains of reality. This, in essence, is why the laws of the universe remain stable and unchanged.

On the basis of logic we cannot know whether history is finite, but we can look at the reality around us and form a picture. Finding a simple rule that explains all observations from fundamental physics makes it highly plausible that history is finite and that in general no completed infinities exist. This is finitism. However, finding ever deeper layers in reality gives the opposite impression, more akin to the Ruliad.
