Infinite happiness and magic

16 May 2026

"The most important problem in any conceivable fantasy world is infinite happiness, but reality carries the magic of its origin within itself."

This sentence presented itself today, while I wondered what the most essential problem might be at various levels — home, world, reality, fantasy. It felt less like a conclusion than like something slowly unfolding, as if it was already lying somewhere waiting to be found.

Where infinite happiness begins to chafe
In our imagination we can effortlessly conjure up worlds in which everything is good, beautiful, and fulfilled. We can stack happiness, layer upon layer, without any visible limit. But the question remains quietly in the background: can such an image truly bear infinity? Infinity is not a large space you simply fill; it is an abyss without measure. Perhaps our imagination falls short here, not because it is poor, but because it remains bounded while trying to encompass the boundless.

The ground that does not explain itself
Reality seems to rest on a foundation that refuses to dissolve, yet does not collapse. The origin is at once understandable and incomprehensible, logical and illogical — a silent paradox that does not ask to be resolved. This is not a weakness, but a given that carries everything. The world proves reliable in its progression, even when its first moment eludes conclusive explanation. As if the deepest ground beneath our feet is firm, precisely because nothing lurks behind it.

A cathedral without a final stone
Perhaps our reality knows only one truly "magical" moment: that of the origin itself. A beginning without reason, which nonetheless sets everything in motion. From there, a strict, reliable order unfolds — no ongoing magic, no interventions from outside. For there is no outside. The void is not a space that encloses, but an absence without form or edge. Precisely because of that, nothing can halt the growth of the universe. Safety, knowledge, and harmony can continue to deepen without endpoint. Happiness too can keep expanding, like a cathedral without a final stone, in which every completion is merely a new beginning.
